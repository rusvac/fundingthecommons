# The Belt

## What makes something a *thing*?

A person isn't one thing. You're a bundle of memories, impulses, habits, emotions, and physical sensations that somehow produce a single action at any given moment. When all of that is working together — when your past experience and your current intention line up enough — you act. You're coherent. You're a unit.

When it breaks down — when you're torn between two choices, when your body wants one thing and your mind wants another, when you can't remember why you started — you fragment. You stop being a unit. The people around you notice. You can't coordinate with others if you can't coordinate with yourself.

This is true for anything that acts in the world. A team that can't agree internally can't function as a team externally. A company with warring departments can't serve customers. An AI agent built from a language model, a memory system, and a set of tools doesn't automatically become a coherent actor — something has to hold those pieces together.

We call that something the belt.

## Bino's Belt

We built an agent called Bino to test this idea. Bino is an autonomous AI agent. Under the hood, it's a collection of parts:

- A reasoning model (Claude Opus for hard decisions, lighter models for routine stuff)
- A memory graph (every person, concept, and conversation is a node — connections form over time)
- A set of tools (APIs, code execution, web access, messaging)
- A planning system that looks forward (what am I trying to do?) and backward (what do I know? who am I?)

None of these parts are Bino. The belt is what makes them Bino. It's the runtime — the harness — that takes all of these components and produces one coherent action at a time.

The belt sits at the present moment. Everything behind it is memory (increasingly abstract the further back you go — from "what just happened" to "who I am"). Everything ahead of it is intention (increasingly abstract the further forward you look — from "what I'm about to do" to "what I'm trying to become"). The belt is where those two directions meet and produce action.

This is the same structure a person has. You act from the intersection of your history and your intentions. When they align, you feel like yourself. When they don't, you feel lost.

## Why This Matters

Before you can coordinate with others, you have to be a coherent unit. Before agents can participate in communities, governance, economies — they need to be *entities* first. Not just models with API keys. Entities with memory, intention, and the ability to act consistently over time.

The belt is what produces that. It turns a pile of AI components into something that can show up, remember what happened yesterday, and follow through on what it said it would do.

That's the foundation. What happens when these entities start coordinating with each other — that's the other half of the story.
